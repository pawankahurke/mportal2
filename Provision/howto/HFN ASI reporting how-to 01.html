How to produce a report or event notification covering one or more sites átop

1) Set up a new user (via the admin module) with access only to the site(s) you want the new user to be able to access (with "Normal" privileges, and no additional rights).

a) Log onto your ASI server (https://asiservername/main)

b) Click on the "Use extended interface" link on the right side of the ASI Dashboard upper pane, below user login and date.

c) In the "tools" navigation bar, click on the "admin" link.

d) Click on the "new user" link located above the table listing users defined on the ASI server.

e) Fill in "name" (eg. acme), "password", and "confirm" fields.

f) In the "Notify mail", and "Report mail" fields enter the e-mail address of the notification and report recipient(s).

g) In the "Sites" list, click on the entry for the site you want the new user to have access to. If you want to select more than one site, press the "Ctrl" key and click the mouse on the entry for each site you want the new user to have access to.

h) Click on the "Create" button at the bottom of the page.

After you have created a user following the above steps, all enabled global reports (both event and asset, legacy and non-legacy), and global event notifications, when executed as per their schedule, will run for each user covering only the sites accessible to that user.

2) You can also produce a report (asset or event), and event notification covering one or more sites by configuring a report (or notification) to cover only selected sites. The following steps describe this procedure for a legacy event report. The steps for defining an asset report, event notification, asset or event sections covering one or more sites are the same except for the fact that you would perform them on the "Asset Reports", "Event Notifications", “Sections” pages respectively.

a) Log onto your ASI server (https://asiservername/main)

b) Click on the "Use extended interface" link on the right side of the ASI Dashboard upper pane, below user login and date.

c) Click on the “legacy reports” link in the “reports” navigation bar found the at upper right-hand corner of any page in the ASI server extended interface. The “Legacy Reports” page will be displayed.

d) In the The “Legacy Reports” page, you will find two links:

  • “Legacy event reports”. Click on this link to access the “legacy” event reports page at https://asiservername/main/event/report.php

  • “Legacy asset reports”. Click on this link to access the “legacy” asset reports page at https://asiservername/main/asset/report.php

  • Click on the "Legacy event reports" link. The "Event Reports" page will be displayed.

e) Retrieve the report you want to configure to cover one or more site:

In the "Name Contains" field in the "Search Options" panel enter one or more words from the report title, and click on the "Search" button.

f) Scroll down to the entry for the report you want to configure to cover one or more site, and click on the "copy" link to the left of the report entry.

g) Change the report title entered in the "Report title" field, as desired.

h) In the "Include" list, click on the entry for the site(s) you want the report to cover. If you want to select more than one site, press the "Ctrl" key and click the mouse on the entry for each site you want the report to cover.

i) Click on the "Add" button at the bottom of the page.

Note that once the new report has been added, it will run either at its scheduled time, or on-demand when you click on the “run” link to the left of the report name in its entry on the “Event Reports” page. In the case of an asset report, you would do the same on the “Asset Reports” page, accessed as described in steps c) and d) above.

Please also note that if you decided to change the schedule of the new report to type “Immediate” (and selected a period for the report to cover), once the report (event or asset) runs, it will be automatically deleted.

How to run a legacy report immediately átop

Suppose you want to run an event report covering the period from July 1, 2006 until August 15, 2006, then you would:

1) Log onto your ASI server (https://asiservername/main)

2) Click on the "Use extended interface" link on the right side of the ASI Dashboard upper pane, below user login and date.

3) Click on the “legacy reports” link in the “reports” navigation bar found the at upper right-hand corner of any page in the ASI server extended interface. The “Legacy Reports” page will be displayed.

4) In the The “Legacy Reports” page, you will find two links:

  • “Legacy event reports”. Click on this link to access the “legacy” event reports page at https://asiservername/main/event/report.php

  • “Legacy asset reports”. Click on this link to access the “legacy” asset reports page at https://asiservername/main/asset/report.php

  • Click on the "Legacy event reports" link. The "Event Reports" page will be displayed.

5) Retrieve the report you want to run immediately:

  • In the "Name Contains" field in the "Search Options" panel enter one or more words from the report title, and click on the "Search" button.

6) Scroll down to the entry for the report you want to run immediately, and click on the "copy" link to the left of the report entry.

7) Change the report title entered in the "Report title" field, as desired.

8) Scroll down to the "Schedule" area of the report configuration page,on the lower right-hand side of the page

9) Select "Immediate" from the "Report cycle" drop-down list.

10) Enter 07/01/06 in the "Start date" field

11) Enter 08/16/06 in the "End date" field

12) Enable the report by selecting "Yes" from the "Enabled" configuration parameter pull-down list, if necessary.

13) Click on the "Add" button at the bottom of the page (left side)

The report will be produced within the next 5-15 minutes (on average). As soon as it is produced, its entry in the "Event Reports" page will be deleted. This is why you make a copy of the report that you want to run immediately (by changing its "Report cycle" to "Immediate").

Alternatively, if the reporting cycle of a report covers the period you want (i.e. the period you want a report to cover is the past month and its "Report cycle" is monthly), then all you need to do is perform steps 1) to 3) described above, and then click on the "run" link to the left of a report's entry. The report will run covering the past X days where X is equal to its reporting cycle. When run immediately in this way, an event report's entry in the "Event Reports" page will not be deleted. Please also note that running an event report via the "run" link works even in the case of reports that are disabled.

Please note that you can follow the procedure described above to produce an asset report immediately. Instead of accessing the "Event Reports" page, you would go to the "Asset Reports" page, accessed as described in steps 3) and 4) above..

How to change/check the schedule of a legacy report átop

The instructions below are for the change of the schedule of an event report. They apply verbatim for asset reports with the exception of step 3) where you should click on the “reports” link in the “assets” navigation bar (https://asiservername/main/asset/report.php)

1) Log onto your ASI server (https://asiservername/main)

2) Click on the "Use extended interface" link on the right side of the ASI Dashboard upper pane, below user login and date.

3) Click on the “legacy reports” link in the “reports” navigation bar found the at upper right-hand corner of any page in the ASI server extended interface. The “Legacy Reports” page will be displayed.

4) In the The “Legacy Reports” page, you will find two links:

  • “Legacy event reports”. Click on this link to access the “legacy” event reports page at https://asiservername/main/event/report.php

  • “Legacy asset reports”. Click on this link to access the “legacy” asset reports page at https://asiservername/main/asset/report.php

  • Click on the "Legacy event reports" link. The "Event Reports" page will be displayed.

5) Retrieve the report whose schedule you want to check or change:

  • In the "Name Contains" field in the "Search Options" panel enter one or more words from the report title, and click on the "Search" button.

6) Scroll down to the entry for the report whose schedule you want to check/modify, and Click on the "edit" link to the left of the report entry

7) Scroll down to the "Schedule" area of the report configuration page, on the lower right-hand side of the page

8) Select "Monthly" from the "Report cycle" drop-down list.

9) Select the hour from the "Hour" pull-down list

10) Select the minute from the "Minute" pull-down list

11) Select the day of the month from the "Day of the month" pull-down list

12) Select "Yes" from the "Enabled" pull-down list in the "Content" section of the "Edit a Report" page (event reports), or in the "Grouping; Sorting Options" section (asset reports) found in the lower right-hand side of the "Edit an Event Report" (or "Edit an Asset Report") page.

13) Click on the "Add" button at the bottom of the page (left side)

How to omit legacy 'zero event' reports átop

1) Log onto your ASI server (https://asiservername/main)

2) Click on the "Use extended interface" link on the right side of the ASI Dashboard upper pane, below user login and date.

3) Click on the “legacy reports” link in the “reports” navigation bar found the at upper right-hand corner of any page in the ASI server extended interface. The “Legacy Reports” page will be displayed.

4) In the The “Legacy Reports” page, you will find two links:

  • “Legacy event reports”. Click on this link to access the “legacy” event reports page at https://asiservername/main/event/report.php

  • “Legacy asset reports”. Click on this link to access the “legacy” asset reports page at https://asiservername/main/asset/report.php

Click on the "Legacy event reports" link. The "Event Reports" page will be displayed.

5) In the "Search Options" panel, select "Enabled" from the "State" search parameter (the second search parameter from the left in the first row)

6) In the "Display Options" panel, select "150" from the "Page Size" parameter, to make sure entries for all enabled event reports are displayed.

7) Click on the "Search" button

8) Click on the "manage" link which you will find just above the “Search Options” panel

9) Click on the "Edit Multiple Event Reports" link

10) Click on the "Check All" button

11) In the Edit Options panel, select "yes" from the "Omit Zero" edit option

12) Click on the "OK" button.
