How to Perform Schedule Related Activities

  • Adding a schedule

  • Copying, or editing a schedule

  • Deleting a Schedule



Adding a schedule átop

  1. Log onto your ASI server (https://asiservername/main). The ASI dashboard will be displayed.

  2. Click on the "Use extended interface" link on the right side of the ASI Dashboard upper pane, below user login and date. The “Welcome” page will be displayed.

  3. Click on the “schedules” link in the “reports” navigation bar found the at upper right-hand corner of any page in the ASI server extended interface. The “Schedules” page will be displayed.

  4. Click on the “add schedule” link found above the “Search Options” panel. The “Add a Schedule” page will be displayed.

  5. Follow the on-screen instructions carefully.

To select an item (e.g. a month, or day), click on it. To de-select it, click on it again.

“Month days” refers to the day of the month you want the schedule to be. For example if you want a schedule for the first day of the month, you would enter 1 in the “Start on day” box. You can have a schedule for multiple days of a month by entering the interval between days of the month in the “repeat every day(s)” box.

When you activate the “Notify me when this schedule fails after failed runs.” options, failure notifications are mailed to the e-mail address specified in the ASI server "support_email" configuration parameter or, if that configuration parameter is empty or unavailable, to support@handsfreenetworks.com

Two type of one-time schedules (defined by entering schedule information in the panel displayed when you click on the “One Time” tab) are possible; ones that run on a date and time in the future which you specify, and ones that will cause a section they are assigned to, to run immediately upon assignment. In either case, you need to define the period of time over which the section you assign the one-time schedule to will retrieve events.

In some cases one-time schedules are not deleted after a report that uses them runs:

  • The schedule is used for other reports, and those other reports have not run yet as a result of that schedule (e.g. They are disabled).

  • The schedule exists but it has not been assigned to any report.

  • The report using the one-time schedule was disabled when the schedule ran.

  • The report using the one-time schedule was disabled when the schedule ran, and the schedule was changed but it will not run again since it has already run once. This can only occur if someone sets up a run-once report, and leaves it disabled, sees the one-time schedule expire, and then changes the schedule and enables the report. In this case, the report will never run.

  1. After you finish defining a schedule, click on the “Add” button found at the top and bottom of the page.

Please note that you can also access the “Schedules” page on the ASI server also via the “manage schedules” link found in the row of links at the top and bottom of the “Reports” page, or the “Sections” pages (main, asset, event, MUM, and summary section pages) (https://asiservername/main/report/sched.php).

Copying, or editing a schedule átop

In order to edit or copy a schedule, please perform the following steps:

  1. Log onto your ASI server (https://asiservername/main). The ASI dashboard will be displayed.

  2. Click on the "Use extended interface" link on the right side of the ASI Dashboard upper pane, below user login and date. The “Welcome” page will be displayed.

  3. Click on the “schedules” link in the “reports” navigation bar found the at upper right-hand corner of any page in the ASI server extended interface. The “Schedules” page will be displayed.

  4. In order to access the schedule you want to edit or copy:

      • Enter one or more character strings from the name of the schedule you want to copy or edit in the "Name Contains" parameter in the "Search Options" panel on the “Schedules” page.

      • If necessary, adjust the value of the “Page Size” parameter in the “Display Options” panel, then

      • Click on the “Search” button.

  1. Click on the “edit” or “copy” link found in the “Action” column for the schedule you want to edit or copy. The “Edit a Schedule” or “Copy a Schedule” page will be displayed

  1. Follow the on-screen instructions carefully.

  2. Click on the “Update” (if you are editing a report), or “Copy” (if you are copying a report) buttons. Note that if you are editing a schedule you do not own, the button label in the “Edit a Schedule” page will be “Add” as you will be adding a copy of the schedule which you will own.

Please note that you can also access the “Schedules” page on the ASI server also via the “manage schedules” link found in the row of links at the top and bottom of the “Reports” page, or the “Sections” pages (main, asset, event, MUM, and summary section pages) (https://asiservername/main/report/sched.php).

Deleting a schedule átop

In order to delete a schedule, please perform the following steps:

  1. Log onto your ASI server (https://asiservername/main). The ASI dashboard will be displayed.

  2. Click on the "Use extended interface" link on the right side of the ASI Dashboard upper pane, below user login and date. The “Welcome” page will be displayed.

  3. Click on the “schedules” link in the “reports” navigation bar found the at upper right-hand corner of any page in the ASI server extended interface. The main “Schedules” page will be displayed.

  4. In order to access the schedule you want to delete:

      • Enter one or more character strings from the name of the schedule you want to delete in the "Name Contains" parameter in the "Search Options" panel on the “Schedules” page.

      • If necessary, adjust the value of the “Page Size” parameter in the “Display Options” panel, then

      • Click on the “Search” button.

  1. Click on the “delete” link found in the “Action” column for the schedule you want to delete. The “Schedule <schedule name> Deletion” page will be displayed.

  2. Click on the “Yes” link. The “Schedule <schedule name> Deletion” confirmation page will be displayed.

Please note that you can also access the “Schedules” page on the ASI server also via the “manage schedules” link found in the row of links at the top and bottom of the “Reports” page, or the “Sections” pages (main, asset, event, MUM, and summary section pages) (https://asiservername/main/report/sched.php).
